# What is Semantic HTML?

**Semantic HTML** means using HTML tags that accurately describe the meaning of the content, rather than just how it looks.

## Comparison

-   **Non-semantic**: `<div id="header">` (Provides no meaning to the browser).
-   **Semantic**: `<header>` (Clearly indicates this is the header).

## Why use it?
1.  **Accessibility**: Screen readers use these tags to navigate the page quickly.
2.  **SEO**: Search engines can easily identify the most important parts of your page (like the `<article>` or `<nav>`).
3.  **Readability**: Makes it easier for other developers (and your future self) to understand your code.
